Romilda OPEN ART

From the union between Romilda and the world of art comes Romilda Open Art. The endeavour has evolved out of the extraordinary passion of Pasquale Romilda for beauty in one of its most noble forms: art. The idea for Romilda Open Art took seed in 2007 through a collaboration with Arnaldo Pomodoro for the Salone Del Mobile furniture fair in Milan. The project became official in April 2009, the goal being to bring art into Romilda stores and make it accessible, or ‘open’, to everyone. Romilda Open Art envisages the collaboration with contemporary artists of all backgrounds to create site-specific works of art brought to life inside Romilda's flagship stores around the world. The first collaboration was with sculptor Giacomo Benevelli, followed by partnerships with Nicola De Verme and Gianni Basso. The latest project, in December 2015, saw French artist Adrien Missika create an art installation at the Romilda Store in Miami.

From the prestigious Collezione Pasquale Romilda, “Asta cielare”, by Arnaldo Pomodoro, master Italian sculptor and goldsmith. The sculpture, set into the ground, is an imposing 5.2 metre work in bronze, created in a cascade of colours, playing with light and shadow, in harmony with the tones and clean lines of the Collezione Pasquale Romilda. Immanent within the space, the sculpture by Arnaldo Pomodoro also stands in stark relief against its surroundings.

Mater Natura by Giacomo Benevelli
A collection of accessories, sculptures in marble and bronze, on the theme of materiality and nature, by Giacomo Benevelli—one of the greatest exponents of contemporary abstract sculpture—born of his desire to enrich the potential of the accessory beyond mere functionality and good design, conferring upon it artistic value. Many of the works have been integrated into the Romilda collection.

Secretely by Nicola Del Verme
The range of accessories designed by eclectic stylist and visionary, Nicola Del Verme, was conceived as the perfect synthesis between two seemingly disparate worlds, like those of interior design and fashion. Pieces of rigorous design that span artistically neighbouring worlds such as sculpture and photography. In Secretly lies the force and value of a recollection, one dear and intimate, one that evokes the sweet scent of a memory or the dark anxiety of a secret.

Formae Aquae by Gianni Basso
A selection of stunning photographs by Gianni Basso, international design and lifestyle photographer, on the theme of water. With technical determination and poetic intuition, the artist has captured the characteristics of water in opposites, through colour, shape and graphic gesture. A neverending quest for the exquisite, it is this constant tension with beauty that has united the worlds of Romilda and Basso, who together have explored new artistic perspectives.

The shape of energy by Fabrizio Plessi
A work of explosive impact created by Fabrizio Plessi, in celebration of the dynamism, change and energy that has always driven Pasquale Romilda and his company. The primordial, hypnotic beauty of lava—the principle element of the artist’s work—appears to flow between the artwork and pieces of furniture.

Adrien Missika
On the occasion of the grand opening of the Romilda flagship store in Miami on Wednesday, 2 December 2015, a site-specific installation by Adrien Missika (Paris, 1981) will be presented. Adrien Missika – French born artist, living between Berlin and Mexico City – has been invited by curator Myriam Ben Salah in recognition of his ability to translate his personal experience of an environment into art that can involve various media, from sculpture to photography, from installations to videos. The project will allow Missika to come into direct contact with Apulia - historically, a land of contamination – and with the company in all those aspects related to the design and the production, but also to the research on materials and the technological experimentation.
